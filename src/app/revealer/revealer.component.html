About Preference Revealer

Preference Revealer sorts a list of items by asking you to make pairwise comparisons. (This level of decomposition isn’t always appropriate, but sometimes it’s invaluable.)

Inspired by Tōhō Sort. Hat tip to pluffei for suggesting a general-purpose version. Many thanks to Knuth for his in-depth discussion of minimal-comparison sorting.

Unlike Tōhō Sort, which uses merge sort, Preference Revealer uses the Ford-Johnson algorithm, a.k.a. merge insertion, as described in Knuth’s The Art of Computer Programming, vol. 3: Sorting and Searching.

The original Preference Revealer code was written by Michael de Cardery (Xomnom) and was updated by David Czeck (czeckd).
